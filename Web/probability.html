Probability

Probability is the measure of how likely an event is to occur. It is expressed as a number between 0 (impossible) and 1 (certain).

Key Concepts

  • Experiment: An action that produces outcomes (e.g., rolling a die).
  • Sample Space (S): The set of all possible outcomes.
  • Event (E): A subset of the sample space.
  • Probability Formula: \( P(E) = \frac{\text{Number of favorable outcomes}}{\text{Total outcomes}} \)

Examples

Example 1: Coin Toss

A fair coin is tossed. What is the probability of getting heads?

Example 2: Rolling a Die

A die is rolled. What is the probability of getting a number greater than 4?

Practice Questions

Q1: Two Coins

Two fair coins are tossed. What is the probability of getting exactly one head?

Q2: Deck of Cards

A card is drawn from a standard deck of 52 cards. What is the probability of getting a heart?
