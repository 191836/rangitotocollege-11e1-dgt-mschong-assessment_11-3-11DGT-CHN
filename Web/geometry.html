Geometry Module

This module introduces basic geometry concepts, worked examples, and practice problems.

1. Angles

An angle is formed by two rays sharing a common endpoint (the vertex). For example, a right angle measures \(90^\circ\).

Example: Complementary Angles

If one angle is \(30^\circ\), find its complementary angle.

Solution:

Complementary angles add up to \(90^\circ\). \(90^\circ - 30^\circ = 60^\circ\). So the other angle is \(60^\circ\).

Practice Problem

If one angle is \(45^\circ\), what is its complementary angle?

\(90^\circ - 45^\circ = 45^\circ\)

2. Area of Shapes

The area of a triangle is given by the formula: \(\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}\).

Example: Triangle Area

Find the area of a triangle with base \(10 \, \text{cm}\) and height \(6 \, \text{cm}\).

Solution:

\(\frac{1}{2} \times 10 \times 6 = 30 \, \text{cm}^2\)

Practice Problem

Find the area of a triangle with base \(8 \, \text{cm}\) and height \(5 \, \text{cm}\).

\(\frac{1}{2} \times 8 \times 5 = 20 \, \text{cm}^2\)

3. Pythagoras' Theorem

In a right triangle: \(a^2 + b^2 = c^2\), where \(c\) is the hypotenuse.

Example: Pythagoras

Find the hypotenuse of a right triangle with sides \(a=3\), \(b=4\).

Solution:

\(c^2 = 3^2 + 4^2 = 9 + 16 = 25 \implies c = 5\)

Practice Problem

A right triangle has legs \(a=5\), \(b=12\). Find the hypotenuse.

\(c^2 = 5^2 + 12^2 = 25 + 144 = 169 \implies c = 13\)
